#### Cultivation Protocol

**Nursery Management**
```
STAGE: SEED TO TRANSPLANT (25-30 days)

Bed preparation:
├── Raised beds: 1m wide × 15cm high × convenient length
├── Soil mix: 2:1:1 (soil:FYM:sand)
├── Treat soil: Carbendazim @ 2g/kg soil
└── Bed area needed: 100 m² per hectare

Sowing:
├── Seed treatment: Thiram @ 2g/kg seed
├── Sowing depth: 1-1.5 cm
├── Row spacing: 10 cm
├── Seed spacing: 2-3 cm
└── Cover with: Fine soil + paddy straw mulch

Nursery care:
├── Irrigation: Light watering twice daily
├── Thinning: Remove weak seedlings at 10 days
├── Hardening: Reduce water 5 days before transplant
└── Ready when: 4-5 true leaves, 15-20 cm height
```

**Main Field Preparation**
```
STAGE: TRANSPLANT TO ESTABLISHMENT (7-10 days)

Land preparation:
├── Ploughing: 2-3 times to fine tilth
├── FYM application: 20-25 t/ha (15-20 days before transplant)
├── Basal fertilizer: NPK as per soil test
└── Bed formation: 90-100 cm bed width, 30 cm furrow

Transplanting:
├── Seedling age: 25-30 days
├── Time: Evening hours (avoid heat stress)
├── Spacing: 60 × 45 cm (determinate) or 75 × 60 cm (indeterminate)
├── Depth: Same as nursery level
├── Population: 27,000-37,000 plants/ha
└── Immediate irrigation: Light watering after transplant
```

**Irrigation Schedule**
```
GROWTH STAGE          FREQUENCY       DEPTH
─────────────────────────────────────────────
Establishment         Daily           Light (5mm)
Vegetative            4-5 days        25-30mm
Flowering             3-4 days        30-35mm
Fruit development     3 days          35-40mm
Ripening              5-6 days        20-25mm

WATER REQUIREMENT: 500-600 mm total crop duration

CRITICAL PERIODS (water stress severely affects yield):
├── Flowering: Causes flower drop
├── Fruit set: Causes blossom end rot
└── Fruit enlargement: Reduces size, cracks
```

**Fertilizer Schedule**
```
TOTAL REQUIREMENT (kg/ha):
├── Nitrogen (N): 120-150 kg
├── Phosphorus (P₂O₅): 60-80 kg
├── Potassium (K₂O): 80-100 kg
└── Based on soil test + expected yield 40 t/ha

APPLICATION TIMING:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Timing              │ N      │ P₂O₅   │ K₂O    │ Method    │
├─────────────────────┼────────┼────────┼────────┼───────────┤
│ Basal (transplant)  │ 30%    │ 100%   │ 50%    │ Broadcast │
│ 20-25 DAT           │ 25%    │ —      │ —      │ Side dress│
│ 40-45 DAT           │ 25%    │ —      │ 25%    │ Side dress│
│ 60-65 DAT           │ 20%    │ —      │ 25%    │ Side dress│
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

MICRONUTRIENT SPRAY (if deficiency):
├── Boron: Borax 0.2% at flowering
├── Calcium: CaCl₂ 0.5% for BER prevention
└── Zinc/Iron: Chelated forms 0.5% as needed
```

### Disease Reference: Tomato

#### Early Blight (Alternaria solani)

**Identification**

| Stage | Symptom | Location |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Initial | Small brown spots (1-2mm) | Lower leaves |
| Progressive | Concentric rings ("target spot") | Expanding from initial spots |
| Advanced | Yellowing around lesions | Leaf margins |
| Severe | Defoliation, stem lesions | Whole plant |
| Fruit | Dark, sunken, leathery lesions | Stem end of fruit |

**Environmental Triggers**

| Factor | Favorable Condition | Risk Level |
|--------|---------------------|------------|
| Temperature | 24-29°C (optimal) | High |
| Humidity | >80% RH | High |
| Leaf wetness | >2 hours continuous | High |
| Plant stress | Nitrogen deficiency | Moderate |
| Crop stage | Flowering onward | High |
| Inoculum | Previous crop debris | High |

**Disease Cycle**
```
PATHOGEN: Alternaria solani (fungus)

SURVIVAL:
├── Infected plant debris in soil (1-2 years)
├── Seed-borne (external contamination)
└── Alternate hosts (other Solanaceae)

SPREAD:
├── Wind-dispersed spores
├── Rain splash
├── Irrigation water
└── Contaminated tools

INFECTION CYCLE: 3-5 days from spore to symptom
GENERATION TIME: 7-10 days per cycle
```

**Integrated Management**

| Strategy | Action | Timing |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **Cultural** | Crop rotation (3-year cycle) | Pre-planting |
| | Remove crop debris | Post-harvest |
| | Avoid overhead irrigation | Throughout |
| | Stake plants for airflow | Vegetative stage |
| | Mulching to prevent splash | Transplant |
| **Biological** | Trichoderma viride @ 2.5 kg/ha | Soil application |
| | Pseudomonas fluorescens spray | Preventive |
| **Chemical** | Mancozeb 75% WP @ 2.5g/L | First sign |
| | Chlorothalonil 75% WP @ 2g/L | Alternating spray |
| | Azoxystrobin 23% SC @ 1ml/L | Severe infection |

**Spray Schedule**
```
PREVENTIVE (when conditions favor disease):

Week    Product             Rate        Interval
────────────────────────────────────────────────
1       Mancozeb 75% WP     2.5g/L      —
2       Chlorothalonil      2.0g/L      7 days
3       Mancozeb 75% WP     2.5g/L      7 days
4       Azoxystrobin 23%    1.0ml/L     7 days

CURATIVE (active infection):

Day     Product             Rate        Notes
────────────────────────────────────────────────
0       Azoxystrobin 23%    1.0ml/L     Systemic
5       Mancozeb 75% WP     2.5g/L      Contact
10      Difenoconazole 25%  0.5ml/L     Systemic
15      Mancozeb 75% WP     2.5g/L      Contact

PHI (Pre-harvest interval):
├── Mancozeb: 7 days
├── Chlorothalonil: 7 days
├── Azoxystrobin: 3 days
└── Difenoconazole: 5 days
```

#### Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans)

**Identification**

| Stage | Symptom | Location |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Initial | Water-soaked spots | Leaf tips, margins |
| Progressive | Grayish-green to brown lesions | Expanding rapidly |
| Advanced | White fungal growth (underside) | Lesion margins |
| Severe | Rapid defoliation, plant collapse | Entire plant |
| Fruit | Brown, firm, rough lesions | Any surface |

**Environmental Triggers**

| Factor | Favorable Condition | Risk Level |
|--------|---------------------|------------|
| Temperature | 15-22°C (optimal) | Very High |
| Temperature | >30°C | Suppressive |
| Humidity | >90% RH | Very High |
| Rainfall | Persistent wet weather | Very High |
| Leaf wetness | >10 hours | Very High |
| Fog/dew | Morning condensation | High |

**Critical Difference: Early vs. Late Blight**

| Feature | Early Blight | Late Blight |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Lesion pattern | Concentric rings | Irregular, water-soaked |
| Lesion color | Brown, dry | Grayish-green, oily |
| Spread speed | Gradual (days) | Rapid (hours) |
| Optimal temp | 24-29°C | 15-22°C |
| White growth | Absent | Present (humid conditions) |
| Fruit symptoms | Stem end | Any location |

**Management**

| Strategy | Action | Timing |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **Monitoring** | Check weather forecast daily | Kharif season |
| | Scout lower canopy every 3 days | Flowering onward |
| **Preventive** | Metalaxyl-M + Mancozeb @ 2.5g/L | Before rainy period |
| | Dimethomorph 50% WP @ 1g/L | 7-day interval |
| **Curative** | Cymoxanil + Mancozeb @ 3g/L | At first symptom |
| | Fenamidone + Mancozeb @ 3g/L | 5-day interval |

### Safety Precautions

```
BEFORE SPRAYING:
├── Read label instructions completely
├── Wear protective equipment (mask, gloves, goggles)
├── Check wind speed (<10 km/h ideal)
├── Avoid spraying in hot sun (>35°C)
└── Inform family members

DURING SPRAYING:
├── Walk backward to avoid contact
├── Do not eat, drink, or smoke
├── Avoid inhaling spray mist
└── Cover nose and mouth

AFTER SPRAYING:
├── Wash hands and face with soap
├── Clean equipment thoroughly
├── Dispose of empty containers safely (triple rinse)
├── Change clothes before entering home
└── Do not re-enter field for 4 hours minimum
```
